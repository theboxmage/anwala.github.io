14 of the most memorable outfits from the Winter Olympics opening ceremony

Caroline Praderio
Feb. 9, 2018, 12:50 PM
253,270
facebook twitter pinterest email copy link

The 2018 Winter Olympics officially kicked off in Pyeongchang, South Korea on Friday.

The February 9 opening ceremony was packed with stunning visuals, drumming and dance performances, and a no-holds-barred fireworks display. The ceremony also featured the traditional parade of nations, during which athletes and coaches from each Olympic delegation marched into the stadium wearing matching outfits.

Here are some of the best and most memorable looks from the ceremony.

View As: One Page Slides

Start Slideshow »

1/

Bermuda's delegation bravely stepped out in Bermuda shorts.

This is a tradition for the team — they also wore the shorts for the opening ceremonies in Sochi and Rio.

2/

The Tongan flag bearer repeated the oily, shirtless look that made him famous during the Rio Games.

Pita Taufatofua competed in taekwondo in the Summer Olympics. Now he's back to compete in cross-country skiing.

3/

Mexican flag bearer German Madrazo wore a mariachi suit.

He's competing in cross-country skiing at the Winter Games.

Source: Pyeongchang 2018

4/

Togo's delegation rocked some of the ceremony's brightest neons.

With just two athletes (an alpine skier and a cross-country skier), Togo's team is one of the smallest at the 2018 Olympics, according to the official Olympics site.

5/

The athletes from Iran looked sleek in their crisp, white coats.

6/

Team USA's heavy jackets may not look special, but inside they're equipped with heaters that last for hours.

The jackets contain a small battery pack for power and can adjust to three different heat settings, Yahoo Sports reported. Each one is worth nearly $2,500.

It's a handy clothing item to have, especially at these Olympics. Temperatures in Pyeongchang have been so low that some athletes' skis are breaking.

7/

The women of team Nigeria looked sharp in trench coat–esque jackets.

8/

New Zealand flag bearer Beau-James Wells looked warm underneath a thick cape.

Wells will be competing in the Olympics with two of his brothers— they're one of 46 sets of siblings who'll be at the games.

9/

Abzal Azhgaliyev, the flag bearer for Kazakhstan, bundled up in fur.

He's competing in speed skating at the Winter Games, according to the Pyeongchang 2018 website.

10/

Each member of the Japanese team wore their scarves in the exact same way. With their bright red coats, it was a striking effect.

11/

These South Korean Olympic champions looked regal when carried their country's flag before the other teams began to march.

12/

The Colombian delegation opted for ponchos over their bright blue coats.

13/

Swedish flag bearer Niklas Edin got to wear a special black and gold parka. (His teammates wore more muted jackets.)

Edin is on Sweden's curling team, according to the site for Pyeongchang 2018.

14/

Finally, each country's delegation was preceded by a standard bearer dressed in a spectacular, sparkling white outfit and headpiece.
