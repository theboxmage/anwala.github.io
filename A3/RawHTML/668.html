Kim Jong Un's sister is seated on next row to US Vice President at awkward opening of Winter Olympic games

Delegates from North Korea and the United States sat just feet apart, despite the nations being almost at war over the past year

As Olympic Games opening ceremonies go, South Korea’s would have won a gold medal for awkwardness.

The historic event saw delegates from North Korea and the United States, sitting just feet apart, despite the nations being almost at war over the past year.

At the event in Pyeongchang , an icy US Vice President Mike Pence sat just one row in front of Kim Yo-jong, the sister and adviser of dictator Kim Jong-un.

She is the first member of North Korea’s ruling family to enter South Korea since the end of the Korean War in 1953.

Her presence among the 35,000-strong crowd, amid freezing temperatures yesterday, indicates a thawing of tensions between two Koreas. Yo-jong also shook hands with South Korea’s leader Moon Jae-in, who is keen to bring about harmony between the two countries.

But the same cannot be said for relations between the North and the US.

The proximity of Yo-jong and ­President Donald Trump ’s second-in-command comes after years of spats between the countries, and threats of nuclear conflict from Jong-un.

Mr Pence cranked up the pressure again yesterday, speaking of a key meeting he held after the ceremony.

He said he was “honoured to meet victims of the oppressive North Korean regime”, adding: “We heard the harrowing stories of defectors who risked life and limb for freedom.”

Winter Olympics 2018

The opening gala marks the 10th time the Koreas have celebrated together at a sporting event since 2000. The athletes wore white to represent the attempts at peace between the nations, which have often come close to conflict.

It came hours after Jong-un flexed his muscles with a military parade in Pyongyang.

At the ceremony, International Olympic Committee president Thomas Bach alluded to Russia being banned from the Games amid a doping scandal. The country was excluded but 168 Russian athletes are entering as independents.

Mr Bach said: “You can only really enjoy the experience if you respect the rules and stay clean. Only then will your lifelong memories be the memories of a true and worthy Olympian.”

On the Koreas’ display of unity, he said: “We are all touched by this wonderful gesture. United in our diversity, we are stronger than all the forces that want to divide us.”

Meet the Winter Olympics athletes

The athletes’ parade included a few hardy participants, a Bermudan delegation who braved the cold in shorts and socks and the Tongan cross-country skier Pita Taufatofua, who sported a skirt and flip-flops. The flame was lit by South Korea’s Olympic heroine Kim Yuna.

Most of Team GB’s record 59-strong squad attended, including our best medal hope, speed skater Elise Christie, and flag-bearer, skeleton racer Lizzy Yarnold.
