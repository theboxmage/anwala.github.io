Olympics Opening Ceremony: If You're Lusting After Team USA Gloves, You'll Want To Check These Out
Allison Olmsted , Contributor As The Gear Diva, I bring function and style to active women’s lives Opinions expressed by Forbes Contributors are their own.
Astis

Long-Cuff Mitten in Byrd (also available as a glove), Long-Cuff Mitten in Crean and Short-Cuff Glove in McKinley (left to right).

Based on the buzz they received when Team USA’s Winter Olympics opening ceremony outfits were revealed, the most coveted item for spectators is going to be the athletes’ rugged, cowboy inspired leather gloves. Once again, Ralph Lauren has been chosen to dress America’s athletes for the opening ceremony, as his company has since 2008, despite the 2012 controversy after it was revealed that the uniforms were made in China (they have since been made in the U.S.A.).

The opening ceremony, which will take place in in an open-air stadium in Pyeongchang, South Korean, will be cold, and forecasts expect it to feel like 15-20 degrees Fahrenheit with the wind chill. So in addition to looking good and being representative of Team USA, the outfits need to be warm - hence the heated jacket and those gloves. If you are inspired and crave a similar and proven pair for yourself, you’ll want to know the story of Astis (and the Ralph Lauren version is not available).

Brad Peterson, the Astis co-founder, was given a custom-made pair of mittens by a friend some years back. The friend traded a Swiss army knife with a Cree woman, who made him the mittens, which he gave to Peterson, an avid skier. Peterson happily skied with them for 12 years, until his sister’s dog, Sadie, ate them (I’m sure it wasn’t Sadie’s fault). After searching high and low, Peterson couldn’t find a replacement, so he decided to improve upon the original, make a new pair, and bring them to the market. He partnered with an equally adventure-focused friend, and after they perfected the mittens, Astis was founded in 2010.

Astis

The Callie Long-Cuff Mitten, the number one seller (left), and a Children’s Mitten in Avanti.

Astis came to my attention when I first saw a pair at the Park City resort in Utah on the hands of Kaylin Richardson, a two-time Olympian, former U.S. Ski Team racer and champion freeskier. They were eye catching, very different from anything else out there, and according to Richardson, really warm. I was immediately taken with them. But as I discovered, the important thing to note is that while they are stunning, with intricate beadwork that may appear delicate, they are made specifically for rugged use, and especially for winter sports such as skiing, snowshoeing, snowmobiling and snowshoeing - yet they’ll also look cool on city streets. Astis now makes gloves as well, and both the mittens and gloves are very warm, and lined with Polartec’s Thermal Pro, which is highly breathable, fast drying, durable and designed to provide warmth without weight. Astis worked with a tannery in the U.S. to inject silicone into the suede during the tanning process to further increase waterproofing. The gloves and mittens are made in the U.S.

Asti

Tough enough for the slopes...

These gloves and mittens will keep your hands warm and look impressive at the same time. Be ready for compliments and questions on lift lines - and everywhere else. Astis has grown its product line greatly in the past eight years, with serval more options than the Long-Cuff Mittens that started it all, but the Caillie, in black, still remains the number one seller. Overall, however, brown and tans are more popular, with the categories of Short-Cuff Mittens and Short-Cuff Gloves second and third in popularity, respectively.

While all the gloves and mitten are lined and treated the same, mittens are naturally warmer then gloves, and the Long-Cuff Mittens are the warmest due to the forearm coverage. They comfortably go over a jacket sleeve, but are meant to be fitted enough to keep snow out.

Dinno Kovic / Dinno Kovic Photography

...yet still awesome in town.

If the Olympics have you feeling patriotic, there are a couple of pairs with flags or eagles in red, white and blue, as well as Canadian and Colorado flag designs. There are countless other colorful designs with colorful, intricate beadworks, and with and without fringe or fur. They are unisex and there are models for kids as well. Astis has also added a suede face mask/neck warmer and beaded bracelets to its product line.
